Marketing Analytics Crosstabs for Polaris Intelligence (APS490Y)

APS490 Course Project, the goal is to build an efficient function that allow Polaris's clients to select and view data for two variables in a cross table for an aggregated selection of postal codes.

Project Overview

I need to contribute to the development and work of a high-performance engineering design team, work with students from different disciplines.
Also, I have to professionally communicate design activities to different audiences using a variety of appropriate media to accomplish milestones.
Our team created formal design proposal, come up with multiple solutions.
Since I am in a multidisciplinary team, I had to accurately assess gaps in skills and knowledge and successfully engage in self-study to fill those gaps.

The Development Process

For the frontend, we used React, HTML, JavaScript, and CSS in PHP files.
  React-beautiful-dnd is used to create drag and drop.
  POST is used to upload and communicate with the backend.
For the backend, we used PostgreSQL and PHP.
  After the variables from the frontend are sent to the backend, the algorithm identifies the types of variables. And calculate its count based on the total count.
  Then it creates a query based on variables, and executed on the database.
  If no errors, the algorithm sends the table to the frontend in JSON format, and stores variables in the database for viewing past table purpose.

Course Overview

APS490 is a unique capstone design course within the Faculty of Applied Science and Engineering (FASE) at University of Toronto. All of the projects in the MCP have an industry client for whom the project represents a real business need. All of the projects are also explicitly multidisciplinary in nature. Successfully completing the project will require skills and knowledge from across multiple engineering disciplines.

This course is intended for exceptional students who are looking for a challenging capstone design experience. Students who would like to work on an MCP project must apply to the Course Coordinator, with admission to the course based on demonstrated skills, knowledge, and experience as an engineering designer within their discipline.

Each MCP project has an associated supervisor who will work with one or more student teams. The supervisor is the primary point of contact for both the client and the teams and is responsible for ensuring that both the client’s and the MCP’s desired outcomes materialize. Supervisors are selected from among the pool of FASE design instructors.
